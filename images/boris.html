current projects

  1. Cortical thickness in temporal lobe epilepsy.
    I am interested in structural changes in the neocortex of epileptic patients. We use automated software developed at the BIC to measure thickness across the entire neocortex.
    Areas of significantly decreased cortical thickness in patients with temporal lobe epilepsy (TLE). Patients were compared to age and sex matched healthy controls. Statistical analysis was carried out using Keith Worsley's SurfStat toolbox for Matlab. After importing thickness data (Y), and the group factor (Fact), we fitted a linear model to assess a difference between groups.
    fact = term(Fact);
    X = 1 + fact;
    slm = SurfStatLinMod( Y, X, avsurf );
    contrast= fact.HC - fact.LTLE;
    slm = SurfStatT( slm, contrast );
    resels = SurfStatResels( slm, mask );

    Figure showing the individual analysis of atrophic patients. We mapped the proportion of atrophic patients (i.e., those with a thickness z-score less or equal to -2) on each vertex.

  2. Cortical connectivity.
    A second interest of mine is to investigate cortical connectivity. We are investigating functional as well as structural connectivity. Figure showing inferred entorhinal cortex connections in healthy controls. Connections were inferred using cortical thickness correlations and correspond well to known anatomical tract tracing data from non-human primates.

  3. Gyrification of the neocortex.
    My colleague Hosung Kim and me are studying the folding of the neocortex. We meausure gyrification such as the gyrification index, the fractal dimension, total sulcal length, and average sulcal depth on MRI images.
