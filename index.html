RESEARCH

MULTIMODAL DESCRIPTIONS OF THE HEALTHY AND DISEASED BRAIN

We integrate structural, functional, and metabolic imaging to indentify basic principles of brain organization, brain development and reveal novel and meaningful disease markers.



NETWORK-LEVEL SUBSTRATES OF COGNITIVE AND AFFECTIVE PHENOTYPES

We employ neuroimaging and network analysis to reveal biological substrates of cognitive and affective phenotypes. Through imaging studies in children, we assess whether the structural and functional maturation of specific anatomical areas underlies cognitive development. We seek to identify novel autism spectrum subtypes using imaging pattern learning.



LONGITUDINAL STUDIES OF BRAIN PLASTICITY AND DISEASE PROGRESSION

Using MRI-based longitudinal designs, we gather evidence for progressive neocortical, mesiotemporal, as well as network-level pathology in drug- resistant epilepsy. Together with the Max Planck Institute in Leipzig, Germany, we participate in a large-scale neuroimaging study that studies the effects of socio-cognitive and affective training on behavioral markers, welbeing, and brain networks.



PREDICTING FUTURE CLINICAL OUTCOMES

To emphasize the translational value of state-of-the-art neuroimaging, we develop individualized diganostic and prognostic routines that combine multi-modal imaging with statistical learning.
